Chapter 1: The Mysterious Potion 🧪

Welcome, young apprentice! You've just arrived at the Academy of Code Magic. Your first lesson begins in the ancient Potion Room, where ingredients float mysteriously in labeled jars.

The wise Master Debugger approaches you with a knowing smile. "To become a true Code Wizard," she says, "you must first learn to store magical ingredients in containers. We call these containers... variables."

🎯 Coding Concept: Variables

A variable is like a magical container that holds information. Just like a jar holds potion ingredients!

Example: playerName = "Alex the Brave"

This creates a container called "playerName" and puts "Alex the Brave" inside it.

Your First Spell! ✨

Master Debugger hands you a glowing crystal. "Store your wizard name in this magical variable," she instructs.

What would you like your wizard name to be?

Chapter 2: The Enchanted Door 🚪

Excellent work, young wizard! Your variable magic impressed Master Debugger.

Now you face a mysterious door with ancient runes. A voice echoes: "Only those who understand the magic of decisions may pass. Answer correctly, and the door opens. Answer wrong, and you must try again."

The door shimmers as runes appear: "When it rains, take an umbrella. When it's sunny, wear sunglasses. This is the magic of... conditional statements!"

🎯 Coding Concept: If/Then Statements

Conditional statements help your program make decisions, just like you do every day!

Example:

if (weather == "rainy") {
  action = "take umbrella"
} else {
  action = "wear sunglasses"
}

The Door's Challenge 🗝️

The door speaks: "A dragon blocks your path. What do you do?"

Chapter 3: The Repeating Spell 🔄

The door creaks open, revealing a room filled with floating magical orbs. Wizard, you've learned variables and decisions - now for the final lesson!

Master Debugger appears beside you. "See those 10 dark orbs? They need to be lit with magic. You could cast 10 separate spells... or learn the ancient art of repetition magic - what we call loops!"

🎯 Coding Concept: Loops

Loops let you repeat actions without writing the same code over and over!

Example:

for (i = 1; i <= 10; i++) {
  lightOrb(i)
}

This lights 10 orbs with just 3 lines instead of 10!

Light the Orbs! 💡

How many orbs should we light with your loop spell?
